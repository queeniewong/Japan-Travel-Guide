Tatami

Tatami is a type of mat used as a flooring material in traditional Japanese-style rooms. Traditionally made using rice straw to form the core, the cores of contemporary tatami are sometimes composed of compressed wood chip boards or polystyrene foam.

I discovered this tatami experience by chance from some advertisements posted in the hostel I lived in Kumamoto. It is also need reservation before you go. The hostel boss is very helpful for making the reservation for me. This is the first time for me to make the tatami mat. Following the teacher's instructions, it was not very difficult to do the mat. It was a interesting experience to know how a tatami is made.

Note:
(1) Teacher can speak only Japanese, but he is nice and will teach you patiently.
(2) The location is not near the railway. About 10mins walk from the Torichosuji Tram Station.
